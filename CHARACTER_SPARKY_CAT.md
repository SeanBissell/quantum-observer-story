## THE CAT

**Name:** Sparky
**Details:**
- One-eyed cat (right eye missing? left eye missing? - you decide)
- Adopted by Steve and Nancy
- Don't know how he lost the eye (shelter cat, already missing when they got him)
- Beloved family pet
- Present in the house during Chapter 1

---

## THE GLITCH: TWO EYES

### **Perfect Early Glitch - Chapter 1**

**Why This Works Brilliantly:**

1. **Subtle and dismissible** - easier to rationalize than a car changing color
2. **Nancy doesn't notice** - which is the KEY
3. **Shows timeline bleed happening to everything, not just Steve**
4. **Emotional anchor** - they love this cat
5. **Visual and undeniable** - you can count eyes
6. **Sets up that Nancy experiences glitches too but doesn't register them**
7. **Foreshadows that ALL reality is shifting, not just human memories**

#### **SPARKY HAS TWO EYES**

Steve is still puzzling over the book when Sparky jumps up on the table.

**Steve:** "Hey buddy. Off the table."

Sparky meows, settles on the table anyway. Typical cat.

Steve reaches to pet him, Sparky purrs.

**Then Steve freezes.**

Sparky is looking at him with **two eyes.**

Both eyes. Perfectly healthy. No scarring where the missing eye should be.

**Steve stares.**

**Steve:** "Nancy?"

**Nancy:** (from kitchen) "Mm?"

**Steve:** "When did Sparky's eye... grow back?"

**Nancy:** (not really listening) "What?"

**Steve:** "His eye. When did—"

He looks again.

**One eye.** The missing eye is missing again. Empty socket, familiar scarring.

**Steve blinks.** Did he just—?

**Steve:** Louder. "Nancy, come here."

**Nancy comes in, dish towel in hand.**

**Nancy:** "What's wrong?"

**Steve:** "Look at Sparky."

**Nancy:** Looks at the cat. "What about him?"

**Steve:** "His... eye."

**Nancy:** "Yes, he has one eye. Like always. What's going on?"

**Steve:** "I... I thought I saw..."

Trails off. This sounds insane.

**Nancy:** Concerned now. "Steve?"

**Steve:** "Nothing. Sorry. Just... tired."

**Nancy:** Studies him. "Are you feeling okay? First the book, now—"

**Steve:** "I'm fine. Really."

But he's not fine. He's staring at Sparky, who stares back with his one good eye.

*Did that just happen?*

---

### **Why Nancy Didn't Notice**

**Key Detail:** Nancy wasn't looking at Sparky when he had two eyes.

She was in the kitchen, only half-listening.

By the time she came in to look, Sparky was back to one eye.

**This establishes:**
- The glitches are FAST (temporary, flickering)
- You have to be looking at the exact moment
- Nancy hasn't been paying attention to the small changes
- Steve is noticing because he's LOOKING

## THEMATIC RESONANCE

### **Why Sparky Works Symbolically**

**Sparky = Acceptance of imperfection**

- He's a one-eyed cat
- They adopted him anyway, love him as he is
- He doesn't need two eyes to be whole
- He's content regardless

**The Glitch = Seeing alternate possibilities**

- What if he HAD both eyes?
- Does that make him "better"?
- Or just different?
- Sparky doesn't care either way

**The Lesson (subtle):**

Just like Sparky can be one-eyed or two-eyed across timelines and still be himself...

Steve can have made different choices and still be himself.

The core remains. The variations are just expressions.

**Schopenhauer:** The thing-in-itself (Sparky's essential cat-ness) vs. the representation (one eye vs two)
