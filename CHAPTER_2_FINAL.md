# CHAPTER 2: THE CONVERGENCE (FINAL VERSION)
## Hero's Journey: Meeting with the Mentor → Crossing the Threshold

**CHAPTER THEME:** "Reality is not singular"
**CHAPTER ARC:** Morning after → Nancy's experience → Timeline shifts accelerate → Understanding deepens → Crossing into the mystery
**TONE:** Disorienting, wonder mixed with fear, partnership forming
**LENGTH:** Approximately 20-25 minutes of reading/interaction

---

## SCENE 1: WEDNESDAY MORNING - THE AFTERMATH (7:30 AM)

### **Waking Up**

Steve wakes to gray light through the curtains. Wednesday morning.

For a moment, everything feels normal.

Then he remembers last night. The park. The other Steve. The explanation.

Timelines converging. Quantum decoherence. Mortality approaching.

He turns his head. Nancy is already awake, staring at the ceiling.

**Steve:** "You didn't sleep."

**Nancy:** "Did you?"

**Steve:** "A little. Maybe."

They lie there in silence.

**Nancy:** "Do you think it was real? Last night?"

**Steve:** "I don't know what else it could be."

**Nancy:** "A very elaborate hallucination?"

**Steve:** "Both of us?"

**Nancy:** Sighs. "No. I guess not."

### **Getting Up**

They get up. Same routine. Coffee. Breakfast.

Except nothing feels routine anymore.

Steve keeps looking at things, waiting for them to change.

The book is still on the shelf. Still his signature inside.

Sparky has one eye. For now.

The car in the driveway is still blue.

**Steve:** "Everything looks normal."

**Nancy:** "For now."

**Steve:** "He said today would be more intense."

**Nancy:** "The other you?"

**Steve:** "Yeah."

**Nancy:** "What did he mean by that?"

**Steve:** "I don't know. But I guess we're going to find out."

### **Nancy's Question**

**Nancy:** Pouring coffee with shaking hands. "Steve... if this is real. If timelines are really converging because you're... because we're..."

She can't say it.

**Steve:** "Because I'm running out of time?"

**Nancy:** "Yeah."

**Steve:** "Then I guess I need to understand it. While I still can."

**Nancy:** "I'm scared."

**Steve:** Goes to her. Takes her hands. "Me too."

**Nancy:** "What if I lose you?"

**Steve:** "You're not going to lose me."

**Nancy:** "You don't know that."

**Steve:** "I know I'm not dying today. Or tomorrow. The other me said we have time."

**Nancy:** "But not infinite time."

**Steve:** "Nobody has infinite time, Nancy. We've never had infinite time."

She looks at him. Tears in her eyes.

**Steve:** "Whatever this is, we face it together. Okay?"

**Nancy:** Nods. "Okay."

---

## SCENE 2: NANCY'S EXPERIENCE (9:00 AM)

### **Steve's Call**

Steve is in his office, trying to research quantum mechanics on his laptop. Most of it is incomprehensible.

His phone rings. Daniel.

**Steve:** Answers. "Hey."

**Daniel:** "Hey Dad. Got your voicemail about the investment thing. You're right, total scam. Thanks for looking at it."

**Steve:** "No problem. That's what dads are for."

**Daniel:** "How are you doing? You sounded... off. In the message."

**Steve:** Doesn't remember leaving a voicemail. But checks his phone.

There it is. Yesterday, 3:00 PM. Two-minute voicemail to Daniel.

He doesn't remember calling.

**Steve:** "I'm fine. Just... busy."

**Daniel:** "Okay. Well, thanks again. Love you."

**Steve:** "Love you too."

Hangs up.

Stares at the phone.

When did he call Daniel? He was dealing with the glitches all afternoon. The photos, the car...

But the call log doesn't lie.

**Another timeline bleeding through.**

### **Nancy in the Kitchen**

Nancy is cleaning up breakfast dishes when it happens.

She's washing a coffee mug.

Looks up at the window.

The backyard is different.

Not drastically. But different.

The Douglas fir on the left—taller. Thicker.

The fence—darker stain.

The garden—different flowers. Roses where there should be rhododendrons.

**Nancy:** Blinks.

Back to normal. The fir is its normal size. Fence is lighter. Rhododendrons.

She sets down the mug carefully.

**Nancy:** "Steve?"

No answer. He's upstairs.

She looks out the window again.

Normal.

Then shifts.

Different yard. Same layout, different details.

Then back.

Then different again.

**Flickering. Like a bad TV signal.**

### **Nancy's Panic**

**Nancy:** Louder. "Steve!"

She backs away from the window.

Looks at her hands. Still her hands.

Looks around the kitchen.

The painting on the wall—their wedding photo—flickers.

Different frame. Then same frame. Then different.

Steve's tie in the photo: blue. Then red. Then blue. Then red.

**Nancy:** "STEVE!"

### **Steve Arrives**

Steve comes running down the stairs.

**Steve:** "What's wrong?"

**Nancy:** Pointing at the window. "The yard. It keeps changing."

**Steve:** Goes to window. Looks.

Normal yard.

Then shifts. Different trees.

Then back.

He sees it too.

**Steve:** "Oh god."

**Nancy:** "It's happening to me now. Not just you."

**Steve:** "He said it would get more intense."

**Nancy:** "This is INTENSE?"

The kitchen flickers.

Different cabinets. Then same cabinets. Different countertop. Same countertop.

Like reality is having trouble deciding what's real.

**Steve:** Takes Nancy's hand. "Breathe. Just breathe."

**Nancy:** "How are you so calm?"

**Steve:** "I'm not. But panicking won't help."

The flickering slows. Stabilizes.

Kitchen back to normal. Yard back to normal.

**Nancy:** Shaking. "What was that?"

**Steve:** "Timeline overlap. Like the other me said. It's accelerating."

---

## SCENE 3: THE SHIFT - STEVE'S OFFICE (10:30 AM)

### **Working Together**

Steve and Nancy are in his office. She won't let him out of her sight.

They're trying to document what's happening. Writing down what they remember vs. what evidence shows.

**List so far:**
- Book: Should be gone, is here
- Sparky: One eye, briefly two
- Patterson: Remembers regatta Steve doesn't remember
- Photos: Both Seattle and marina on same day
- Car: Silver in memory, blue in reality
- Wedding flowers: Both red and white memories
- Move date: Both August and September memories
- Daniel's voicemail: Don't remember making it
- Backyard: Flickering between variations

**Steve:** "It's not random. It's not just chaos."

**Nancy:** "What do you mean?"

**Steve:** "Every variation is connected to a choice. The book—I chose to donate it. The car—I chose silver. The regatta—I chose not to race. But in other timelines, I chose differently."

**Nancy:** "So we're seeing timelines where you made different choices?"

**Steve:** "Yeah. All overlapping with this one."

**Nancy:** "But why? Why now?"

**Steve:** "The other me said... when you get close to the end, they start converging. All the branches coming back together."

**Nancy:** Quiet. "I hate that explanation."

**Steve:** "Me too. But it makes sense."

### **The Office Shifts**

Steve is typing notes when his office changes.

Not flickers. Full shift.

Different desk. Different chair. Different books on shelf.

Same room. But arranged differently.

He's sitting at a standing desk. His current desk is traditional.

**Steve:** "Nancy—"

**Nancy:** "I see it."

They're both frozen, looking around.

**Steve:** Stands slowly. Looks at the desk.

Papers on it. His handwriting. But not notes he wrote.

Picks one up. Reads:

> "Timeline Log - Day 3
> Experiencing increased overlap. Nancy is aware now. Good. She needs to know.
> Meeting with Steve-B again tonight. He says the convergence is faster than usual.
> Not sure how much time I have left to understand this."

**Steve:** "This is... this is me. But not me. From a timeline where this started earlier."

**Nancy:** "How much earlier?"

**Steve:** "Three days, according to this."

The office flickers.

Back to normal. His regular desk. His notes.

The paper in his hand is gone.

**Steve:** "Did you see that?"

**Nancy:** "The paper? Yes."

**Steve:** "It said I'm on day three. But for us, it's day two."

**Nancy:** "Different timelines are at different points?"

**Steve:** "Maybe. Time isn't linear if quantum mechanics is involved. Some versions of me might be ahead. Some behind."

**Nancy:** "This is hurting my brain."

**Steve:** "Mine too."

---

## SCENE 4: THE NEIGHBORHOOD WALK (11:30 AM)

### **Needing Air**

**Nancy:** "I need to get out of this house."

**Steve:** "Want to go for a walk?"

**Nancy:** "Yes. Please."

They get jackets. The rain has stopped. Overcast but dry.

Walk down their street in Newcastle. Familiar route.

**Nancy:** "Is it safe? To go outside?"

**Steve:** "I don't know. But I can't sit inside waiting for reality to change again."

**Nancy:** Takes his hand. "Okay. Together."

They walk.

### **The Shift Happens**

They're passing the neighbor's house—the Pattersons—when it happens.

The world shifts.

Not flickers. Complete transition.

Everything changes.

The street is different. Same houses, but different colors. Different cars in driveways.

The Pattersons' house is blue, not yellow.

Their own house behind them—different roof.

**Nancy:** Stops. "Steve."

**Steve:** "I see it."

They're not in their timeline anymore.

### **Different Timeline - Observation**

They stand there, looking around.

Everything is slightly off.

**Steve:** "We should go back. To the house."

**Nancy:** "Which house? Our house in this timeline might not be ours."

**Steve:** "Good point."

They stand in the middle of the street.

A car drives by. Different model. Driver doesn't look at them.

**Steve:** "Are we... visible? In this timeline?"

**Nancy:** "I don't know. This is insane."

**Steve:** "Let's just... observe. See what's different."

### **Walking in the Alternate Timeline**

They walk down the street slowly.

Everything is familiar but wrong.

Like a dream of their neighborhood.

**Nancy:** "Look. The Johnsons' house. They have a different garden."

**Steve:** "Different car too. That's a Tesla. They drive a Subaru."

**Nancy:** "In our timeline."

**Steve:** "Yeah."

They turn the corner.

The park at the end of the street—same location, different playground equipment.

**Steve:** "Same place, different choices. Different purchases. Different lives."

**Nancy:** "How many timelines are there?"

**Steve:** "Infinite? Every choice branches. Every decision creates a new one."

**Nancy:** "That's... that's too big to think about."

### **Meeting Someone**

A woman is jogging toward them. Neighbor—Sarah Chen. Lives two houses down.

In their timeline, Sarah is a teacher.

This Sarah is wearing scrubs. Nurse? Doctor?

She jogs past. Nods hello.

Doesn't recognize them. Or does, but differently.

**Nancy:** "Did she—"

**Steve:** "She knows us. But not as us. As whoever we are in this timeline."

**Nancy:** "This is so confusing."

**Steve:** "Yeah."

### **The Shift Back**

They're standing by the park when everything shifts back.

**Pop.** Like a soap bubble.

Their timeline. Their neighborhood.

Pattersons' house is yellow again. Their house has its normal roof.

Johnsons' garden is their rhododendrons.

**Nancy:** Exhales. "We're back."

**Steve:** "Or we were never gone. Maybe we just... observed a parallel timeline."

**Nancy:** "While standing in ours?"

**Steve:** "Quantum superposition. Existing in multiple states simultaneously."

**Nancy:** "I really hate quantum mechanics."

**Steve:** Laughs despite everything. "Me too."

---

## SCENE 5: LUNCH - GROUNDING (12:30 PM)

### **Back Home**

They make lunch. Sandwiches. Simple. Normal.

Trying to pretend things are normal.

Sparky winds around their legs, meowing for food.

**Steve:** Feeds him. "At least you're consistent."

Sparky eats. One eye. Still just one eye.

**Nancy:** "What do we tell Sean and Daniel?"

**Steve:** "Nothing. Not yet."

**Nancy:** "They should know—"

**Steve:** "And say what? 'Hi sons, your dad is experiencing quantum timeline convergence because he's approaching death'?"

**Nancy:** Flinches. "Don't say it like that."

**Steve:** "Sorry. But what else do I say?"

**Nancy:** "I don't know. But they'll notice something's wrong."

**Steve:** "We'll tell them. When I understand it better. When I have answers."

**Nancy:** "And if you don't?"

**Steve:** "Then we tell them anyway. But not today."

### **The Question**

**Nancy:** Eating her sandwich mechanically. "Steve... the other you. Last night. He said you'd have to choose."

**Steve:** "Choose what?"

**Nancy:** "Which version to be. When they all collapse back together."

**Steve:** "Yeah."

**Nancy:** "What does that mean?"

**Steve:** "I don't know."

**Nancy:** "What if you choose wrong? What if you choose a timeline where I'm not there? Where Sean and Daniel are different? Where—"

**Steve:** "Nancy. Stop."

She's spiraling. He can see it.

**Steve:** Takes her hands. "Whatever choosing means, I'm not choosing away from you. You're in every timeline. The other me said that. I married you in every variation."

**Nancy:** "But different versions of me."

**Steve:** "Still you."

**Nancy:** "How do you know?"

**Steve:** "Because the core of who you are—that doesn't change based on my choices. You're you."

**Nancy:** Wants to believe that.

**Steve:** "We're going to figure this out. And whatever happens, we're together. Okay?"

**Nancy:** "Okay."

But she's scared. He can see it.

So is he.

---

## SCENE 6: THE AFTERNOON - MULTIPLE SHIFTS (2:00 PM - 4:00 PM)

### **Acceleration**

The shifts are happening more frequently now.

Every 20-30 minutes, something changes.

Sometimes small: A book title on the shelf. The brand of coffee in the cupboard.

Sometimes bigger: The color of the living room walls. The type of TV they own.

Steve and Nancy start treating it like a game. Documenting changes.

**Game: Spot the Difference**

**Nancy:** "The couch was blue. Now it's gray."

**Steve:** "The painting. That was a landscape. Now it's abstract."

**Nancy:** "Your watch. That's different."

**Steve:** Looks at his wrist. Digital watch. But he wears analog.

Looks again. Analog.

**Steve:** "It's changing faster."

### **The Pattern**

**Steve:** "Wait. I'm seeing a pattern."

**Nancy:** "What?"

**Steve:** "Every change relates to a choice I made. The couch—we bought that together. Blue in this timeline, gray in another where I picked differently. The watch—I chose analog, but almost got digital."

**Nancy:** "So you're seeing the echoes of every decision?"

**Steve:** "Yeah. All the branches. All the variations."

**Nancy:** "How many decisions have you made in seventy-two years?"

**Steve:** "Millions."

**Nancy:** "So infinite timelines."

**Steve:** "Functionally, yeah."

**Nancy:** "How do you process that?"

**Steve:** "I don't know. But I'm going to have to."

### **Steve Sees Himself**

Around 3:00 PM, Steve is in the kitchen getting water.

Looks out the window.

Sees himself in the backyard.

Another Steve. Wearing different clothes. Older? Younger? Hard to tell.

The other Steve is just standing there, looking at the trees.

**Steve:** "Nancy—"

**Nancy:** Comes over. "What?"

**Steve:** Points.

But the other Steve is gone.

**Steve:** "There was another me. In the backyard."

**Nancy:** "From another timeline?"

**Steve:** "Must be."

**Nancy:** "Is he... is he seeing us too?"

**Steve:** "Maybe. Maybe we're all bleeding into each other."

**Nancy:** "That's terrifying."

**Steve:** "Yeah."

### **Nancy Sees Herself**

Around 4:00 PM, Nancy is in the bathroom.

Looks in the mirror.

Sees herself.

But different. Older. Grayer. More lines.

A Nancy from a timeline where things were harder.

**Nancy:** Gasps.

Blinks.

Her normal reflection.

Then the older Nancy again.

Then normal.

**Nancy:** Touches her face. Real. Solid.

But the older Nancy in the mirror looks back.

Sad eyes.

**Older Nancy:** (mouthed, no sound) "Hold on to him."

Then gone. Normal reflection.

**Nancy:** Backs out of the bathroom, shaking.

Finds Steve.

**Nancy:** "I saw me. An older me. In the mirror."

**Steve:** "What did she—"

**Nancy:** "She said to hold on to you."

**Steve:** Pulls her close. "I'm right here."

**Nancy:** "For now."

---

## SCENE 7: EVENING - THE OTHER STEVE RETURNS (6:00 PM)

### **The Doorbell**

They're making dinner when the doorbell rings.

Steve and Nancy look at each other.

**Nancy:** "You expecting anyone?"

**Steve:** "No."

They go to the door.

Steve opens it.

**It's him. The other Steve from last night.**

Standing on their porch like it's perfectly normal.

**Other Steve:** "Can I come in? We need to talk."

### **Inside**

Nancy stares at the other Steve.

Seeing him in daylight, in their house, is surreal.

Same face as her husband. But not her husband.

**Other Steve:** "Hi Nancy. I'm—"

**Nancy:** "Another Steve. I know. This is so weird."

**Other Steve:** "Yeah. It doesn't get less weird."

**Steve:** "What are you doing here?"

**Other Steve:** "Checking in. You've been experiencing accelerated convergence today."

**Steve:** "How do you know?"

**Other Steve:** "Because I am too. And we're connected. Same consciousness, different branches."

### **Explanation Continues**

They sit in the living room. Two Steves and Nancy.

Sparky jumps on the other Steve's lap. Doesn't care that there's two of them.

**Other Steve:** Pets Sparky. "One eye in this timeline too?"

**Steve:** "Yeah."

**Other Steve:** "Two in mine. We got him before the injury."

**Nancy:** "This is so bizarre. Talking about our cat in different timelines."

**Other Steve:** "That's what this is. Every choice, every moment, splits. Most of the time, you never see the other branches. But now..."

**Steve:** "The walls are breaking down."

**Other Steve:** "Exactly."

### **Why It's Accelerating**

**Nancy:** "Why is it getting faster?"

**Other Steve:** Hesitates.

**Nancy:** "Tell us."

**Other Steve:** Looks at Steve. "Because you're reaching the convergence point. Where all the timelines come back together."

**Steve:** "When?"

**Other Steve:** "I don't know exactly. Could be days. Could be weeks. But soon."

**Nancy:** "And then what?"

**Other Steve:** "Then you integrate. All the versions, all the choices, all the experiences. They collapse back into one."

**Steve:** "And I become... what? A combination of all of them?"

**Other Steve:** "Something like that. You'll remember all the paths. All the lives. And you'll understand."

**Nancy:** "Understand what?"

**Other Steve:** "What it all meant. Why the choices mattered. Why every path was valid."

### **The Schopenhauer Moment (Subtle)**

**Steve:** "But I only lived one life. This one."

**Other Steve:** "Did you? You lived all of them. You're living all of them right now. You just only have conscious access to one timeline at a time. Until now."

**Steve:** "So which one is real?"

**Other Steve:** "All of them. None of them. 'Real' is just the timeline you're observing."

**Nancy:** "That sounds like philosophy, not physics."

**Other Steve:** "Maybe they're the same thing."

**Echoes Schopenhauer: The world as Will and Representation**

### **What Comes Next**

**Steve:** "So what do I do? Just ride it out?"

**Other Steve:** "No. You explore. You meet the other versions. You see what you were in all the timelines. You learn."

**Steve:** "Learn what?"

**Other Steve:** "That's up to you. Every version learns something different."

**Steve:** "Have you? Learned something?"

**Other Steve:** Pauses. "Yeah. I learned that the life I thought was a mistake wasn't. That choosing differently wasn't wrong. It was just different."

**Nancy:** "What did you choose differently?"

**Other Steve:** "I stayed in Seattle. Turned down the Bellevue job. Smaller company. Less money. Different trajectory. For years, I thought I made the wrong choice. Especially when I saw your life—" gestures to Steve "—the success, the stability. But now I see... it wasn't wrong. Just different."

**Steve:** "Are you happy? In your timeline?"

**Other Steve:** "Happiness is complicated. But I'm at peace. Mostly."

**Schopenhauer: Acceptance over striving**

### **The Warning**

**Other Steve:** "Over the next few days, you're going to meet more. More versions of yourself. Some you'll like. Some you won't. Some will show you lives you wish you'd lived."

**Steve:** "And?"

**Other Steve:** "Don't get lost in them. Don't start thinking one is 'better' than yours. They're all just variations. None is more real than another."

**Steve:** "Easier said than done."

**Other Steve:** "I know. But it's important. The suffering doesn't come from the choices you made. It comes from thinking they should have been different."

**Pure Schopenhauer: Suffering from the Will's dissatisfaction**

**Nancy:** "How do we know which is our timeline? If they're all bleeding together?"

**Other Steve:** "You don't. And eventually, that stops mattering."

---

## SCENE 8: AFTER HE LEAVES (7:30 PM)

### **Processing**

The other Steve leaves. Just walks out into the evening and disappears.

Steve and Nancy sit in silence.

**Nancy:** "I don't know how to feel about this."

**Steve:** "Me neither."

**Nancy:** "Part of me is terrified. Part of me is... curious?"

**Steve:** "Yeah. Me too."

**Nancy:** "He seemed sad. The other you."

**Steve:** "He chose differently. Maybe he has regrets."

**Nancy:** "Do you?"

**Steve:** "Regrets?"

**Nancy:** "About Bellevue. About the choices you made."

**Steve:** Thinks. "Sometimes I wonder. What if I'd stayed in Seattle? What if I'd taken more risks? What if I'd—"

**Nancy:** "What if you'd married someone else?"

**Steve:** Looks at her sharply. "No. That's not—"

**Nancy:** "You said you married me in every timeline. But different versions of me."

**Steve:** "The core is still you."

**Nancy:** "Is it? Or am I just telling myself that?"

### **The Fear**

**Nancy:** "What if you see a timeline where you're happier? Where you made better choices? Where your life is... better than this one?"

**Steve:** "Nancy—"

**Nancy:** "What if you choose that one? When the convergence happens? What if you integrate into a version that doesn't include this me?"

**Steve:** "That's not going to happen."

**Nancy:** "You don't know that."

**Steve:** Takes her hands. "I know that in seventy-two years, the best choice I ever made was you. Every other choice—the job, the city, the car, the boat, all of it—none of it matters compared to you."

**Nancy:** Tears in her eyes. "You can't know that for sure."

**Steve:** "Yes I can. Because even now, with reality falling apart, you're the thing that makes sense. You're the thing I'm sure about."

**Nancy:** "Promise me. Promise you won't choose away from me."

**Steve:** "I promise."

They hold each other.

Outside, reality shifts. The streetlight flickers between different styles.

But inside, they're solid.

For now.

---

## SCENE 9: NIGHT - THE INVITATION (10:00 PM)

### **Getting Ready for Bed**

They're exhausted. Emotionally drained.

Steve is brushing his teeth when his reflection in the mirror changes.

Not Nancy's older self. Another Steve.

Younger. Mid-40s maybe.

**Reflection Steve:** Speaks. "Tomorrow."

**Steve:** "What?"

**Reflection Steve:** "Tomorrow you'll see more. Be ready."

**Steve:** "Ready for what?"

**Reflection Steve:** "For all of it. All the versions. All the choices."

The reflection shifts back to normal. Just Steve, 72, tired.

He stares at himself.

*Tomorrow.*

### **In Bed**

**Nancy:** "You okay?"

**Steve:** "Saw another me. In the mirror."

**Nancy:** "What did he say?"

**Steve:** "Tomorrow. More timelines. More versions."

**Nancy:** "Are you ready?"

**Steve:** "No. But I don't think I have a choice."

**Nancy:** "We'll face it together."

**Steve:** "Yeah."

They lie in the dark, holding hands.

Outside, the world shifts and flickers.

Timelines bleeding together.

Reality uncertain.

But they have each other.

And for now, that's enough.

---

## SCENE 10: MIDNIGHT - THE THRESHOLD (12:00 AM)

### **The Shift**

Steve can't sleep.

Gets up. Goes downstairs.

Living room is dark except for streetlight through the window.

Sparky is on the couch. Looks up at him with one eye.

**Steve:** Sits next to the cat. Pets him.

**Steve:** "You don't care about any of this, do you?"

Sparky purrs.

**Steve:** "Lucky cat."

The room shifts.

Not a flicker. Full transition.

Different living room. Same house, different furniture.

Sparky is gone. Different cat on the couch. Orange tabby. Two eyes.

Steve stands, turns.

**A woman is standing in the doorway.**

Not Nancy. But familiar.

**Woman:** "Steve?"

**Steve:** "Who—"

**Woman:** "It's me. Lisa."

Lisa. He knows that name. Where—

College. Lisa Chen. His girlfriend before Nancy.

They dated for two years. Almost got engaged. Then broke up. She moved to California.

He hasn't thought about her in decades.

But here she is. In this living room. In this timeline.

**Lisa:** "Are you okay? You look like you've seen a ghost."

**Steve:** "I... I don't..."

This is a timeline where he married Lisa, not Nancy.

Where they built a life together.

Where Nancy is somewhere else. Or nowhere.

**Steve:** "I have to go."

**Lisa:** "Steve, what's wrong?"

**Steve:** Backs toward the stairs.

The room shifts back.

His living room. His furniture. Sparky on the couch, one eye.

Nancy's voice from upstairs: "Steve? You okay?"

**Steve:** Breathes. "Yeah. I'm fine."

But he's not fine.

He just saw a life without Nancy.

And it terrified him.

### **Return to Bed**

He goes back upstairs. Nancy is awake.

**Nancy:** "Bad dream?"

**Steve:** "Something like that."

He gets into bed. Holds her tight.

**Nancy:** "You're shaking."

**Steve:** "I saw a timeline. Where I didn't choose you."

**Nancy:** Goes still. "And?"

**Steve:** "And I hated it."

**Nancy:** "Even if that life was good?"

**Steve:** "It wasn't good. Because you weren't in it."

She holds him back.

Two people clinging to each other while reality fractures around them.

### **The Crossing**

**Steve:** "Tomorrow, the other me said it gets more intense."

**Nancy:** "More than today?"

**Steve:** "Apparently."

**Nancy:** "I don't know if I can handle more."

**Steve:** "We don't have a choice. This is happening whether we're ready or not."

**Nancy:** "Then we face it."

**Steve:** "Together."

**Nancy:** "Together."

Outside, the world shifts one more time.

Different street. Different sky. Different stars.

Then back to normal.

**The threshold has been crossed.**

Tomorrow, Steve will step fully into the quantum maze.

He'll meet his other selves.

He'll see his other lives.

And he'll begin to understand what it all means.

But tonight, he holds Nancy.

And tries to remember which timeline is his.
