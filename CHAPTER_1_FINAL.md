# CHAPTER 1: THE GLITCH (FINAL VERSION)
## Hero's Journey: Ordinary World → Call to Adventure → Refusal of Call

**CHAPTER THEME:** "Something is wrong with reality"
**CHAPTER ARC:** Normal morning → Escalating impossibilities → Denial crumbles → Cannot be denied
**TONE:** Grounded, increasingly unsettling, dry humor masking growing fear
**LENGTH:** Approximately 15-20 minutes of reading/interaction

---

## SCENE 1: ORDINARY WORLD (8:00 AM)

### **Setting**
Steve and Nancy's home in Newcastle, Washington. Comfortable two-story suburban house. Tuesday morning, early March. Rain pattering on windows—typical Pacific Northwest weather.

### **Opening Image**

Steve wakes to the sound of rain and the smell of coffee brewing. Nancy's already up, her side of the bed cool. Morning light filters gray through the curtains.

He stretches, feels the familiar ache in his shoulders—seventy-two years of living. Not bad, all things considered. Could be worse.

Checks his phone on the nightstand: 8:03 AM. No rush. Retirement has its perks.

### **Character Establishment: Steve**

Gets dressed: comfortable jeans, gray fleece pullover. Looks in the mirror briefly—still himself. Gray hair, still has most of it. Lines on his face. Each one with a story.

"Not bad for seventy-two," he says to his reflection.

Heads downstairs.

### **Character Establishment: Nancy**

Nancy is in the kitchen, pouring coffee. She's wearing her reading glasses, has the Seattle Times open on the tablet propped on the counter.

**Nancy:** "Morning. Sleep okay?"

**Steve:** "Like a rock. What's the news?"

**Nancy:** "Same as yesterday. World's still spinning."

**Steve:** "Good to know. Wouldn't want it to stop without warning."

She hands him a mug—his favorite, the one Sean gave him that says "World's Okayest Dad."

**Steve:** Takes a sip. "Perfect. You're a goddess."

**Nancy:** "I know. Don't forget it."

**Their dynamic:** Easy banter. Forty-five years of marriage. Comfortable. Solid.

### **Ordinary World Details**

Steve sits at the kitchen table. Through the window: their backyard, Douglas firs swaying in the rain. His bike in the garage, waiting for better weather. The boat's winterized down at the marina.

**Steve:** "Thinking about going for a ride later if the rain lets up."

**Nancy:** "Your knees will punish you."

**Steve:** "My knees punish me anyway. Might as well earn it."

He scrolls through his phone. Email from his former coworker about a golf outing. Text from Daniel from yesterday:

> "Dad, got a question about this investment thing. Looks like BS but wanted your take."

Steve smiles. Daniel, always the skeptic. Like his old man.

**Steve:** "Should call Daniel back. He's asking about some investment scam."

**Nancy:** "He trusts your instincts."

**Steve:** "Or he just likes having someone to blame when it goes wrong."

**Nancy:** Smiles. "That too."

### **Establishing Normalcy**

Everything is routine. Familiar. Safe.

- Coffee tastes right
- Rain sounds normal
- Nancy's presence is comfortable
- The house is exactly as it should be
- Tuesday morning, nothing special
- Life is good

**Steve thinks:** Not bad. Retired, healthy enough, good wife, grown kids doing well. Could be worse. Could be a lot worse.

Then he notices the book.

---

## SCENE 2: THE FIRST GLITCH - THE BOOK (8:20 AM)

### **The Discovery**

Steve gets up to refill his coffee. Passes the living room bookshelf.

Stops.

There's a book on the shelf that shouldn't be there.

**"Gödel, Escher, Bach: An Eternal Golden Braid" by Douglas Hofstadter.**

He recognizes it immediately. The cover—that distinctive interlocking pattern. He owned this book. In college. 1973.

But he doesn't own it anymore.

### **Steve's Reaction - Curiosity**

**Steve:** Calls to kitchen. "Nancy, did you buy a book recently?"

**Nancy:** "What book?"

**Steve:** Pulls it from the shelf. "Gödel, Escher, Bach."

**Nancy:** Appears in doorway, dish towel in hand. "What about it?"

**Steve:** "Did you buy it?"

**Nancy:** "No. Why?"

**Steve:** "It's on our shelf."

**Nancy:** Shrugs. "So? We have lots of books."

**Steve:** "But I haven't seen this in... decades."

### **The Detail That's Wrong**

Steve opens the book.

Inside cover, in faded blue ink: **"Steve Morrison, Spring 1973"**

His handwriting. His signature. From fifty-three years ago.

He flips through pages. Margin notes in his hand. Underlining. His younger self's thoughts preserved in pen.

Page 42: *"This is either brilliant or complete bullshit. Need coffee to decide."*

He remembers writing that. 2 AM in his dorm room, trying to understand recursive functions.

But he gave this book away.

### **Steve's Memory**

He remembers clearly:

Early '90s—'92, maybe? The year's a bit fuzzy now, but the feeling isn't. Moving from Seattle to Bellevue for the job. Packing the apartment. Nancy, young Sean, maybe Daniel on the way. Boxes everywhere.

This book, along with dozens of others, donated to a library sale. He specifically remembers putting it in the box. Thought about keeping it, decided not to. Too heavy, literally and metaphorically. New life, new city, new job. Forward, not backward.

He remembers donating it.

So why is it here?

### **The Rationalization**

**Steve:** "This is... I gave this away. When we moved to Bellevue."

**Nancy:** "Maybe you bought another copy?"

**Steve:** "It has my signature. From college. Inside."

**Nancy:** Looks at it. Sees the signature. "Huh. Maybe it never made it into the donation box?"

**Steve:** "I remember putting it in."

**Nancy:** "Steve, that was almost forty years ago."

**Steve:** "I remember."

**Nancy:** Gently. "You're seventy-two. Sometimes memories get fuzzy."

### **Steve's Internal State**

He wants to argue. But she's got a point. Forty years ago. Maybe he's misremembering. Maybe he kept it and forgot. Maybe it's been on the shelf this whole time and he just never noticed.

Memory is tricky at any age.

But it feels wrong.

**Steve:** "Yeah. Maybe you're right."

**Nancy:** "You okay?"

**Steve:** "Fine. Just... weird."

She goes back to the kitchen. Steve stands there holding the book.

Stares at his young handwriting.

*"This is either brilliant or complete bullshit."*

Maybe memory is both.

He puts the book back on the shelf.

**First seed of doubt planted.**

---

## SCENE 3: THE SECOND GLITCH - SPARKY'S EYES (8:45 AM)

### **The Cat**

Steve is back at the kitchen table, trying to read news on his tablet but distracted by the book thing.

**Meow.**

Sparky jumps up onto the table. Gray tabby cat, twelve pounds of attitude.

**Nancy:** "Sparky, off."

Sparky ignores her. Settles on the table, tail swishing.

**Steve:** "Hey buddy."

Reaches to pet him. Sparky purrs, leans into his hand.

Steve looks at the cat.

### **The Moment**

Sparky is looking at him.

With two eyes.

Both eyes. Two clear, healthy, bright green eyes.

Steve freezes.

Sparky has one eye. Right eye missing. Lost it before they adopted him two years ago. Shelter didn't know how. Infection, injury, born that way—doesn't matter. One-eyed cat. They loved him anyway.

But right now, looking at Steve:

**Two eyes.**

### **Steve's Reaction - Confusion**

**Steve:** Stares. Doesn't move.

Counts: Left eye. Right eye. Both there. Both clear.

**Steve:** "Nancy—"

**Nancy:** From kitchen, not listening. "Mm?"

**Steve:** "Sparky's—"

He blinks.

**One eye.**

Right eye socket empty, scarred. Familiar. Normal.

Left eye staring at him.

Steve's hand is frozen mid-pet.

**Steve:** "What—"

### **Did He See That?**

Sparky meows, pushes his head against Steve's hand. Wants more petting.

Steve pets him automatically, mind elsewhere.

Did he just... did Sparky have two eyes for a second?

He looks closely. One eye. Right socket scarred, closed, empty. Same as always.

**Steve:** "Nancy, come here."

**Nancy:** Comes to doorway. "What's wrong?"

**Steve:** "Look at Sparky."

**Nancy:** Looks at the cat on the table. "He's on the table again. Bad cat."

**Steve:** "No, his... his eye."

**Nancy:** "Yes, he has one eye. Like always. What about it?"

### **The Doubt**

**Steve:** "I thought... for a second, I thought I saw..."

How does he say this? "I thought your one-eyed cat had two eyes"?

**Nancy:** More concerned now. "Saw what?"

**Steve:** "Nothing. Just... tired, I guess."

**Nancy:** Studies him. "Steve, you're scaring me a little. First the book, now... are you feeling okay?"

**Steve:** "I'm fine."

But he's not fine.

He looks at Sparky. Sparky looks back with his one good eye, totally unconcerned.

**Nancy:** "Maybe you should take it easy today. Skip the bike ride."

**Steve:** "Yeah. Maybe."

She goes back to the kitchen, but she's worried. He can tell.

Steve sits there, Sparky purring under his hand.

*Did I just imagine that?*

He's seventy-two. Is this how it starts? Seeing things that aren't there? Forgetting things? Misremembering?

**Deeper anxiety beginning.**

---

## SCENE 4: THE THIRD GLITCH - PATTERSON'S CONVERSATION (11:15 AM)

### **Getting the Mail**

Steve needs air. Needs to clear his head.

**Steve:** "Going to get the mail."

**Nancy:** "Okay. Take your phone."

Like he's a child. But he doesn't argue.

Rain has stopped. Overcast, but dry. Cool March air.

He walks down the driveway in his jacket. Neighbor across the street—Jim Patterson, retired engineer—is checking his mailbox too.

**Patterson:** Waves. "Steve! Morning!"

**Steve:** "Hey Jim."

They meet at the street. Same routine, couple times a week.

### **The Conversation**

**Patterson:** "How's the boat? Ready for racing season?"

**Steve:** "Boat's good. Still winterized though. Season doesn't start till May."

**Patterson:** Confused look. "I meant last weekend. The regatta."

**Steve:** "What regatta?"

**Patterson:** "The... the Spring Preview race? Saturday? You texted me about it."

**Steve:** "Jim, I didn't race last weekend."

**Patterson:** "Steve, we talked about it Saturday morning. You called me from the marina."

**Steve:** "No, I didn't. Nancy and I went to Seattle Saturday. Anniversary dinner."

**Patterson:** Now looking concerned. "Steve... are you feeling alright?"

### **The Escalation**

**Steve:** "Jim, I'm serious. I didn't race. I was in Seattle with Nancy all day Saturday."

**Patterson:** Pulls out his phone. "You texted me Saturday afternoon. Look."

Shows Steve his phone.

There's a text message from Steve's number:

> "Placed 3rd at the regatta! Boat handled beautifully in that wind. Your jib adjustment tip made the difference. Thanks!"

Timestamp: Saturday, 2:47 PM.

**Steve:** Stares at the phone.

**Steve:** "I didn't send that."

**Patterson:** "That's your number."

**Steve:** Pulls out his own phone. Scrolls to Saturday.

There's the text. In his sent messages. To Jim Patterson.

He sent it.

But he didn't race. He was in Seattle. He remembers. Anniversary dinner at the Italian place Nancy loves. They had wine. Took a walk. Got home around 10 PM.

He didn't send this text.

But there it is.

### **The Growing Fear**

**Steve:** "Jim, I... I don't know what this is."

**Patterson:** Genuinely worried now. "Steve, maybe you should see someone. Doctor. Just to check—"

**Steve:** "I'm not sick."

**Patterson:** "I'm not saying sick, I'm saying... you know. Memory issues. It happens. My uncle—"

**Steve:** "I'm fine."

**Patterson:** "Steve—"

**Steve:** "I have to go."

Walks back to his house, leaving Jim standing there looking worried.

### **Internal Crisis**

Steve's heart is pounding.

That text is in his phone. He can see it.

But he didn't send it. He didn't race. He was in Seattle.

Unless...

Unless he wasn't?

Unless he's misremembering?

But he remembers Seattle. The restaurant. The wine. Nancy's laugh. Walking in the rain.

That happened.

But the text is real too.

**Both can't be true.**

His hands are shaking slightly.

*What's happening to me?*

---

## SCENE 5: THE FOURTH GLITCH - THE PHOTOS (2:30 PM)

### **Talking to Nancy**

Steve has been quiet all afternoon. Nancy notices.

**Nancy:** "You're brooding."

**Steve:** "I'm not brooding."

**Nancy:** "You absolutely are. What happened with Jim?"

Steve tells her about the text. The regatta. Jim's memory vs. his memory.

**Nancy:** Frowns. "That's... strange."

**Steve:** "Do you remember Saturday? Seattle? Dinner?"

**Nancy:** "Of course. We had the carbonara and you complained about the parking."

**Steve:** Relief. "Okay. Yes. So I wasn't at a regatta."

**Nancy:** "But the text..."

**Steve:** "I know."

### **Nancy Checks Her Phone**

**Nancy:** "Wait, let me check my photos from Saturday."

She pulls out her phone. Opens Photos. Scrolls to Saturday.

Goes still.

**Nancy:** "Steve..."

**Steve:** "What?"

She shows him her phone.

Photos from Saturday.

Some are from Seattle: The restaurant. The street. Nancy smiling, Steve in the background.

But others:

The marina. The boat. Steve at the helm, wind in his hair, racing. Clear sky. Water sparkling.

**Mixed together. Both sets of photos. Same day.**

### **Steve's Response**

**Steve:** Takes her phone. Scrolls through.

Seattle, marina, Seattle, boat, restaurant, racing.

**Steve:** "This isn't possible."

**Nancy:** "Did someone... hack our phones? Add fake photos?"

**Steve:** "Who would do that? And why?"

**Nancy:** "I don't know, but there has to be an explanation."

**Steve:** "What if there isn't?"

**Nancy:** "Don't say that."

**Steve:** "Nancy, what if I'm... what if something's wrong with me?"

### **Nancy's Fear**

She sees it in his eyes. Real fear. Not just confusion. Fear.

**Nancy:** "Let's call Daniel. He can recommend a neurologist. Just to rule things out."

**Steve:** "I don't need a neurologist."

**Nancy:** "Steve, you're seeing things—"

**Steve:** "I'm not SEEING things. The photos are THERE. The text is THERE. Jim REMEMBERS it. But I was in Seattle. I REMEMBER Seattle."

**Nancy:** "Then something's wrong with the phones—"

**Steve:** "And Jim's phone too? And his memory? And the book this morning? And—"

He stops. Takes a breath.

**Steve:** Quieter. "I'm scared."

### **Nancy's Comfort**

She goes to him. Takes his hands.

**Nancy:** "We'll figure it out. Whatever this is."

**Steve:** "What if I'm losing my mind?"

**Nancy:** "You're not."

**Steve:** "How do you know?"

**Nancy:** "Because I know you. And even if something is happening, we'll deal with it. Together."

He holds her hands. Anchors himself.

**Steve:** "Okay."

But neither of them is convinced.

---

## SCENE 6: THE FIFTH GLITCH - THE BLUE CAR (6:00 PM)

### **Evening**

Steve has been in his office all afternoon, trying to work on hobby stuff, anything to distract himself. Can't focus.

Nancy made dinner. They eat mostly in silence.

**Nancy:** "Maybe we should call Sean and Daniel. Tell them what's happening."

**Steve:** "And say what? 'Hey sons, your dad might be going senile at seventy-two'?"

**Nancy:** "Don't talk like that."

**Steve:** "What else do you call it?"

### **Looking Out the Window**

Steve gets up, goes to the window. Looks out at the driveway.

His car is there.

**Blue Toyota Highlander. 2004.**

Wait.

He looks closer.

**It's red.**

Deep red. Not blue. Red.

Same car. Same model. Same license plate. But red.

### **The Breaking Point**

**Steve:** "Nancy."

**Nancy:** "What?"

**Steve:** "What color is my car?"

**Nancy:** Comes to window. Looks. "Red. Why?"

**Steve:** "It's blue."

**Nancy:** "Steve, your car has always been red."

**Steve:** "It's BLUE. I bought it blue twenty years ago. We went to the dealership together."

**Nancy:** "We bought it red."

**Steve:** "No, I remember BLUE."

**Nancy:** "We bought it blue."

**Steve:** "No—"

He goes to the door, opens it, walks out into the driveway.

Rain is starting again. He doesn't care.

Stands in front of his car.

**Deep red Toyota Highlander.**

He opens the door. Interior is his: coffee mug in holder, registration in glove box, his sunglasses on the dash.

License plate: His.

But it's BLUE.

### **Nancy Follows**

**Nancy:** "Steve, come inside. You're getting wet."

**Steve:** "I bought this car silver. I remember. I wanted the silver one. You said it looked sleek."

**Nancy:** "I said the blue looked sleek."

**Steve:** "No."

**Nancy:** "Steve—"

**Steve:** "I remember SILVER."

He touches the hood. Cold, wet, blue metal.

**Steve:** "This isn't happening."

But it is happening.

### **The Admission**

Steve stands there in the rain, looking at his red Highlander that should be blue, holding his phone with photos from a race he didn't sail, thinking about a book he gave away that's on his shelf and a cat who had two eyes for just a second.

**Steve:** Quietly. "Something is very, very wrong with reality."

**Nancy:** "Or something's wrong with—"

**Steve:** Looks at her. "Don't say it."

**Nancy:** "We need to get you checked out."

**Steve:** "And what are they going to find? 'Sorry sir, you're experiencing quantum timeline shifts'? There's no medical condition that explains photos appearing on our phones or—"

He stops.

### **The Realization**

Maybe he's not going crazy.

Maybe something else is happening.

Something impossible.

But what?

**Steve:** "What if I'm not sick? What if this is... something else?"

**Nancy:** "Like what?"

**Steve:** "I don't know. But I'm going to find out."

They go back inside, both soaked.

Neither of them knows what to think.

---

## SCENE 7: THE SIXTH GLITCH - MEMORIES COLLIDING (7:30 PM)

### **Adding Another Layer**

Steve is in the living room, towel-dried, staring at the bookshelf.

Nancy brings him tea. He takes it absently.

**Nancy:** "Talk to me."

**Steve:** "I'm trying to remember. Clearly. With certainty."

**Nancy:** "Remember what?"

**Steve:** "The Bellevue move. Early '90s. When I took that job."

**Nancy:** "What about it?"

### **The Memory Test**

**Steve:** "We moved from Seattle to Bellevue. I remember packing. You were pregnant with Sean. We moved in August."

**Nancy:** "September."

**Steve:** "What?"

**Nancy:** "We moved in September. I remember because my mother came to help and complained about the heat."

**Steve:** "It was August."

**Nancy:** "Steve, it was September."

**Steve:** Closes his eyes. Tries to see it clearly.

He remembers: Summer. August. Hot. Moving boxes. Nancy very pregnant, due in October.

But he also remembers: September. Cooler. Nancy's mother there. Due in November.

**Both memories.**

**Steve:** "Oh god."

**Nancy:** "What?"

**Steve:** "I remember both. August and September. Both."

### **Nancy's Test**

**Nancy:** Getting worried. "Okay, let's try something else. Our wedding. What color were the flowers?"

**Steve:** "White roses."

**Nancy:** "Red roses. They were red."

**Steve:** "No, I remember white—"

But he does remember red. Nancy insisted on red. Her favorite.

But he also remembers white. Elegant. Simple. White roses.

**Steve:** "Red. And white. Both."

**Nancy:** "Steve—"

**Steve:** "I remember both, Nancy. How is that possible?"

She doesn't answer. Can't answer.

---

## SCENE 8: THE NOTE (8:30 PM)

### **Finding It**

Steve needs something solid. Something that makes sense.

Goes back to the bookshelf. Picks up "Gödel, Escher, Bach" again.

Opens it randomly, looking for... what? Answers?

A piece of paper falls out.

Clean. White. Folded.

Not yellowed. Recent.

He picks it up.

Unfolds it.

### **The Message**

Written in his own handwriting:

> Steve,
>
> This is real. You're not sick. You're not crazy. You're not losing your mind.
>
> I know because I'm you. A different you. A version of you from a timeline where you made different choices.
>
> I know you remember standing in the rain outside the Bellevue office. Twenty-three minutes. Deciding whether to take the job. Nobody saw you. You never told anyone.
>
> I know because I stood there too. But I chose differently.
>
> The timelines are overlapping. You're experiencing quantum decoherence. I can explain, but not here. Not like this.
>
> Tonight. Midnight. Gene Coulon Memorial Park, by the water.
>
> Come alone. You'll understand why when you see me.
>
> — Steve

### **Steve's Reaction**

He reads it twice. Three times.

The handwriting is his. Perfect match.

The detail—standing in the rain, deciding—that's real. He did that. Outside the office. Stood there getting soaked, wrestling with the choice. New job, new city, new life. Scary. Exciting. Uncertain.

Nancy was waiting at home. He never told her he stood there doubting.

Nobody knows that but him.

**How does this note know?**

### **Showing Nancy**

**Steve:** "Nancy."

**Nancy:** "Yeah?"

**Steve:** "Read this."

Hands her the note.

She reads. Face goes pale.

**Nancy:** "This is someone's idea of a sick joke."

**Steve:** "Look at the handwriting."

**Nancy:** Looks closer. "It's... it looks like yours."

**Steve:** "It IS mine."

**Nancy:** "Then you wrote it. Forgot you wrote it. Maybe earlier today when you were—"

**Steve:** "I didn't write this. But whoever did knows something they shouldn't."

**Nancy:** "Like what?"

**Steve:** "Like me standing in the rain. I never told anyone about that. Not you, not anyone."

**Nancy:** "Then how—"

**Steve:** "The note says: 'I'm you. A different you.'"

**Nancy:** Stares at him. "You can't be serious."

**Steve:** "I'm going."

---

## SCENE 9: NANCY'S OBJECTION (9:00 PM)

### **The Argument**

**Nancy:** "Absolutely not."

**Steve:** "I have to."

**Nancy:** "Steve, this could be anything. A prank. Someone messing with you. Or—"

**Steve:** "Or what?"

**Nancy:** Quieter. "Or you wrote it yourself and don't remember."

### **Steve's Conviction**

**Steve:** "I know what's happening sounds impossible. But Nancy, look at everything today. The book. Sparky. Jim. The photos. The car. My memories splitting. Something is WRONG."

**Nancy:** "Then we go to the hospital—"

**Steve:** "And tell them what? That my car changed colors? That I remember our wedding flowers as two different colors? They'll think I'm insane."

**Nancy:** "I'm scared."

**Steve:** "Me too. But this note... whoever wrote it knows something. And they say they can explain."

**Nancy:** "What if it's dangerous?"

**Steve:** "It's a park. Public place."

**Nancy:** "At midnight!"

**Steve:** "I'm going."

### **Nancy's Fear**

She looks at him. Really looks at him.

Sees: Her husband of forty-five years. The man she knows better than anyone.

And sees: Fear. Confusion. But also determination.

**Nancy:** "What if you don't come back?"

**Steve:** "I'll come back."

**Nancy:** "Promise?"

**Steve:** "Promise."

She doesn't want him to go. But she knows he will. That's who he is. When something doesn't make sense, he finds the answer.

Even if the answer is impossible.

**Nancy:** "Then I'm coming with you."

**Steve:** "The note says alone."

**Nancy:** "I don't care what the note says."

**Steve:** "Nancy—"

**Nancy:** "I'm not letting you go meet some mysterious stranger who claims to be you at midnight alone. Either I come, or you don't go."

### **The Compromise**

**Steve:** Thinks. "Okay. Come. But... stay in the car. Let me talk to them first. If it's safe, I'll signal you."

**Nancy:** "What signal?"

**Steve:** "I'll wave. If I wave, come over. If I don't..."

**Nancy:** "If you don't?"

**Steve:** "Then call 911 and get out of there."

**Nancy:** Not happy. But nods. "Okay."

---

## SCENE 10: WAITING (9:00 PM - Midnight)

### **The Long Evening**

They try to watch TV. Can't focus.

Steve keeps looking at the note.

Nancy keeps checking her phone, like the time will pass faster.

**Nancy:** "Maybe we shouldn't go."

**Steve:** "We're going."

**Nancy:** "Maybe this is all some elaborate—"

**Steve:** "We're going."

Sparky jumps on Steve's lap. One eye staring up at him.

Steve pets him. Grounds himself.

**Steve:** "You still just have one eye, right buddy?"

Sparky purrs.

**Steve:** "Good. Let's keep it that way."

### **11:30 PM**

They get in the car.

The red Highlander.

Steve still hates that it's red.

Drive in silence.

Rain picks up again. Windshield wipers rhythmic. Hypnotic.

**Nancy:** "What if this person... this 'other you'... what if they're dangerous?"

**Steve:** "Then we leave."

**Nancy:** "Just like that?"

**Steve:** "Just like that."

But he doesn't think he will. Because he needs answers.

And whoever wrote that note has them.

---

## SCENE 11: GENE COULON PARK - ARRIVAL (11:55 PM)

### **The Park**

Gene Coulon Memorial Beach Park. On Lake Washington, Renton.

Dark. Rainy. Empty parking lot.

Street lights reflecting off wet pavement.

Water lapping at the shore in the distance.

Nobody else here. Why would there be? Midnight, raining, Tuesday.

Steve parks. Leaves the engine running.

**Steve:** "Stay here."

**Nancy:** "Steve—"

**Steve:** "Please."

She nods. Reluctant.

**Steve:** "If anything feels wrong—"

**Nancy:** "I'll call 911 and leave. You already said."

He kisses her. Quick. Grounding.

**Steve:** "I'll be right back."

Gets out of the car.

### **Walking to the Water**

Rain is steady. Not pouring, but persistent.

Steve walks toward the water. Hands in jacket pockets.

Cold. March cold. Seattle cold. The kind that gets into your bones.

His breath visible.

Water ahead. Dark. Reflections of lights from across the lake.

He stops at the shore. Looks around.

Nobody.

Checks his watch: 11:58 PM.

Waits.

### **Midnight**

Footsteps behind him.

Steve turns.

Someone walking out of the mist and rain.

Same height. Same build. Same age.

Wearing different clothes—darker jacket, jeans.

Closer.

Street light catches the face.

**It's him.**

Another Steve. Same face. Same gray hair. Same seventy-two years.

Walking toward him like this is perfectly normal.

Like meeting yourself at midnight in the rain happens every day.

**Steve stands frozen.**

The other Steve stops a few feet away.

### **First Words**

**Other Steve:** "Thanks for coming."

His voice. Same voice.

**Steve:** Can't speak. Stares.

**Other Steve:** "I know this is... a lot."

**Steve:** "What... who..."

**Other Steve:** "I'm you, Steve. From the timeline where you didn't take the Bellevue job. Where you stayed in Seattle. Made different choices. But still you."

**Steve:** This is insane.

**Other Steve:** "Is it? After today? The book? The car? The memories splitting?"

**Steve:** How does he know—

Oh. Because he's experiencing it too.

**Steve:** "This isn't possible."

**Other Steve:** "And yet, here we are."

### **The Proof**

**Steve:** "Prove it. Tell me something only I would know."

**Other Steve:** Nods. "Your mother's middle name was Lenore. Nancy called you 'sailor boy' when you were dating and you hated it but never told her. Daniel's graduation speech made you cry and you pretended you had allergies. Your father died before you could tell him you forgave him for missing your wedding."

Steve goes cold.

All true. All private.

**Steve:** "How—"

**Other Steve:** "Because I have the same memories. We diverged at that decision. Everything before that, identical. After that... different paths."

**Steve:** "Why is this happening?"

**Other Steve:** "That's what I'm here to explain."

---

## SCENE 12: CHAPTER 1 ENDING

### **Nancy's Moment**

Back in the car, Nancy is watching through the rain-streaked windshield.

She sees Steve talking to someone.

The someone is... she squints.

Looks exactly like Steve.

**Nancy:** Whispers. "Oh my god."

She grabs her phone. Should she call 911? Should she—

Her phone screen flickers.

The background photo—her and Steve at the beach last summer—changes.

Different beach. Different day. But still them.

Then back to the original.

Then different again.

**Nancy:** Stares at her phone. Hands shaking.

It's happening to her too.

### **Steve's Decision**

**Other Steve:** "I know you have questions. Hundreds of them. I'll answer what I can. But first, you need to understand what's happening."

**Steve:** "Then tell me."

**Other Steve:** "Walk with me."

**Steve:** Glances back at the car. Nancy's silhouette.

**Other Steve:** "She'll be safe. This will take a few minutes."

**Steve:** Hesitates.

Then follows.

They walk along the shore, two versions of the same person, in the rain at midnight.

### **The Beginning of Understanding**

**Other Steve:** "You know quantum mechanics? Superposition? The observer effect?"

**Steve:** "Vaguely."

**Other Steve:** "Every choice you make collapses probability into reality. Left or right. Yes or no. Go or stay. Each choice branches timeline. You chose left, and a universe where you chose right splits off."

**Steve:** "Theoretical."

**Other Steve:** "No. Real. And normally the timelines never interact. Quantum decoherence keeps them separate. You live in one, never seeing the others."

**Steve:** "But now—"

**Other Steve:** "Now they're bleeding together. Overlapping. You're experiencing multiple timelines simultaneously."

**Steve:** "Why?"

**Other Steve hesitates.**

**Other Steve:** "Because we're seventy-two, Steve."

**Steve:** "What does that have to do with—"

**Other Steve:** "Because there's less time ahead than behind. Because the timelines are converging."

**Steve:** Cold runs through him. "You're saying I'm—"

**Other Steve:** "Not tomorrow. Not this year. But eventually. And when it gets close... the walls between timelines get thin. You start seeing all the paths you took. All the versions of yourself."

**Pause. Rain. Water sounds.**

**Steve:** "I'm dying?"

**Other Steve:** "We're all dying. Eventually. This is... this is what happens before. You get to see. All of it. Every choice. Every path. It's a gift."

**Steve:** "Doesn't feel like a gift."

**Other Steve:** "Not yet."

## FINAL BEAT: THE INVITATION

**Other Steve:** "Over the next few days, you're going to experience more. More timelines. More versions. More choices you made and didn't make."

**Steve:** "How do I stop it?"

**Other Steve:** "You don't. You accept it. You learn from it. And eventually... you integrate it."

**Steve:** "What does that mean?"

**Other Steve:** "It means you get to understand your whole life. Every possibility. Every path. And then you choose."

**Steve:** "Choose what?"

**Other Steve:** "Which version to be when they all collapse back together."

**Steve:** "And if I don't want to choose?"

**Other Steve:** Looks at him. "You will. When you understand what this really means."

**Pause.**

**Other Steve:** "Go home, Steve. Be with Nancy. Rest. Tomorrow... tomorrow it gets more intense."

**Steve:** "Wait—"

**Other Steve:** "I'll find you again. When you need me."

**Steve:** "How will you—"

**Other Steve:** Smiles slightly. "We're the same person. I'll know."

Turns. Walks back into the mist.

Gone.

Steve stands there in the rain.

Alone again.

But everything has changed.

## CHAPTER 1 CLOSES

Steve walks back to the car.

Nancy gets out, runs to him.

**Nancy:** "Are you okay? Who was that? I saw—"

Doesn't interrupt.

Not anymore.

What does that mean?

What comes next?

He holds Nancy's hand in the dark.

And waits for morning.
